How to Convert Hourly Wage to Annual Salary

The basic formula is simple:

Annual Salary = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week × Weeks per Year

For a full-time worker (40 hours/week, 52 weeks/year), this is just: Hourly Rate × 2,080. So $25/hour equals $25 × 2,080 = $52,000/year. If you include any overtime or subtract unpaid vacation weeks, the math changes.

Pay Periods Explained

  • Weekly: Paid every week (52 paychecks/year). Common in hourly jobs.
  • Biweekly: Paid every 2 weeks (26 paychecks/year). Most common in US private sector.
  • Semi-monthly: Paid twice per month (24 paychecks/year), typically on the 15th and last day.
  • Monthly: Paid once per month (12 paychecks/year). Common in executive and teaching jobs.

Note that biweekly and semi-monthly are not the same. Biweekly means 26 paychecks, while semi-monthly means 24. This matters because a biweekly paycheck is slightly smaller than a semi-monthly one (for the same annual salary).

Gross vs. Net Pay

This calculator shows gross pay — what you earn before taxes and deductions. Your actual take-home pay (net pay) will be lower due to:

  • Federal income tax (10% to 37% marginal rate)
  • Social Security tax (6.2% on wages up to $168,600 in 2024)
  • Medicare tax (1.45%, plus 0.9% additional for high earners)
  • State and local income taxes (varies by state)
  • Health insurance premiums
  • Retirement contributions (401k, pension)
  • Other deductions (union dues, garnishments, HSA, etc.)

As a rough rule of thumb, expect your net pay to be about 70-80% of your gross pay, depending on your tax bracket and state.

Factoring in Unpaid Time Off

Hourly workers typically only get paid for hours actually worked, so unpaid vacation weeks reduce total annual income. If you take 2 weeks of unpaid vacation per year, enter 50 weeks instead of 52 in the calculator above.

For example, $25/hour × 40 hrs/week × 52 weeks = $52,000/year with no time off. If you take 2 weeks unpaid, that drops to $25 × 40 × 50 = $50,000/year.

Including Overtime in Annual Calculation

Overtime at 1.5x can add significantly to your annual income. At $20/hour with 10 hours of weekly overtime:

  • Regular pay: $20 × 40 × 52 = $41,600/year
  • Overtime pay: $20 × 1.5 × 10 × 52 = $15,600/year
  • Total: $57,200/year (a 37.5% boost)

Keep in mind that consistent overtime isn't guaranteed and may fluctuate with business demand.

FAQ

How many work hours are in a year?

2,080 hours for a standard 40-hour/week full-time job with no unpaid time off (40 × 52 = 2,080).

What's the difference between biweekly and semi-monthly?

Biweekly means 26 paychecks per year (every 2 weeks). Semi-monthly means 24 paychecks per year (twice a month). An annual salary divided 26 ways produces smaller paychecks than divided 24 ways.

Is this calculator accurate for part-time workers?

Yes. Just enter your actual weekly hours (e.g., 20 or 25) instead of 40. The calculator scales the annual amount accordingly.

Does this include taxes?

No. This shows gross income only. Taxes and other deductions will reduce your take-home pay by approximately 20-30% depending on your situation.

How do I convert an annual salary back to hourly?

Divide the annual salary by 2,080 (for full-time). So $60,000/year ÷ 2,080 = $28.85/hour equivalent.
