New York State Overtime Laws

The New York State Labor Law and Minimum Wage Order regulations govern overtime for most private-sector workers in NY. In most respects, New York mirrors the federal FLSA — overtime at 1.5x after 40 hours per workweek — but New York also adds several unique protections that can increase your paycheck.

The 40-Hour Weekly Rule

Non-exempt employees in New York must be paid at 1.5 times their regular rate for all hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek. Unlike California, New York does not require daily overtime — only weekly. A "workweek" is any fixed, recurring 168-hour period.

New York Minimum Wage 2026

New York has different minimum wages depending on region, and this matters for overtime because the exempt salary threshold is tied to the minimum wage. As of 2025:

  • NYC, Long Island, Westchester: $16.50/hour
  • Rest of New York State: $15.50/hour
  • Tipped food service workers: Lower cash wage with tip credit
  • Fast food workers: Special minimum wage may apply

NY Exempt Salary Threshold

New York has a higher exempt employee salary threshold than federal law. To be exempt from overtime in NY under the executive or administrative exemption, as of 2025:

  • NYC, Long Island, Westchester: $1,237.50/week ($64,350/year)
  • Rest of NY State: $1,161.65/week ($60,405/year)
  • Federal threshold: $684/week ($35,568/year)

This means many workers who would be exempt under federal law are still entitled to overtime under New York State law. Always use whichever threshold is higher.

Spread of Hours Rule

New York has a unique "spread of hours" rule that doesn't exist in federal law. If an employee's workday spans more than 10 hours from start to finish (including meal breaks and unpaid time), the employer must pay one additional hour at the minimum wage.

Example: You work a split shift from 8:00 AM to 11:00 AM and 4:00 PM to 8:00 PM (7 hours worked, but 12 hours from start to end). Your employer owes you 7 hours of wages plus 1 additional hour at minimum wage for the spread-of-hours premium.

Call-In Pay (Show-Up Pay)

If you report to work as scheduled and your employer sends you home early, New York may require you to be paid a minimum number of hours at the applicable rate. The exact rules depend on the industry (retail, hospitality, etc.) under the applicable Wage Order. Generally, you must be paid for at least 4 hours or your scheduled shift, whichever is less.

Residential Employees

Residential employees (those who live in the employer's home) are subject to different overtime rules in New York:

  • Overtime applies after 44 hours per week instead of 40
  • Other state labor protections still apply

Farm Workers

As of 2020, New York began phasing in overtime for farm workers. The current weekly threshold is set by the NY Commissioner of Labor and has been decreasing over time, with a goal of reaching 40 hours. Check the NY DOL website for the current year's threshold.

Examples: Calculating NY Overtime

Example 1: Jessica works 50 hours in a Brooklyn restaurant at $18/hour:

  • Regular pay: $18 × 40 = $720
  • Overtime: $18 × 1.5 × 10 = $270
  • No spread of hours triggered
  • Total: $990

Example 2: Miguel works a split shift in NYC from 6 AM to 10 AM and 5 PM to 9 PM (8 hours worked, 15-hour spread) at $17/hour:

  • Regular pay: $17 × 8 = $136
  • Spread of hours premium: 1 hour × $16.50 minimum wage = $16.50
  • Total for that day: $152.50

Example 3: A salaried administrative employee earning $55,000/year in Manhattan:

  • Federal exempt threshold: $35,568 — she would be exempt
  • NY (NYC) exempt threshold: $64,350 — she is NOT exempt
  • Under NY law, she must receive overtime for hours over 40/week

Filing a Wage Claim in New York

If your NY employer isn't paying overtime correctly, you can file a claim with the New York State Department of Labor (NYSDOL) Division of Labor Standards. New York has a 6-year statute of limitations for wage claims — significantly longer than the federal 2-3 year window. You can recover:

  • Back wages
  • 100% liquidated damages (doubling recovery)
  • Interest
  • Attorney's fees and costs

FAQ: New York Overtime

Does New York require daily overtime?

No. Unlike California, New York only requires overtime after 40 hours in a workweek. However, spread-of-hours rules can add extra pay for long days.

What is the NY minimum wage for overtime?

Overtime is calculated based on your regular rate, not the minimum wage. If you earn above minimum wage, your overtime rate uses your actual hourly rate × 1.5.

Are tips included in my overtime rate?

In New York, tipped food service workers must be paid overtime based on the full NY minimum wage (not the lower cash wage), minus the allowable tip credit.

How long do I have to file a NY wage claim?

Six years from the date the wages were due. This is much longer than the federal 2-3 year FLSA statute of limitations.
