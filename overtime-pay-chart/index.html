How to Use These Charts

Find your hourly rate in the leftmost column, then read across to find the rate or total earnings that match your situation. For example:

  • Quick rate lookup: Earning $25/hour, you make $37.50/hour during overtime.
  • Weekly OT calculation: Earning $20/hour with 10 overtime hours, you'll earn an extra $300 that week.
  • Annual OT planning: Earning $30/hour with consistent 10 hours of overtime weekly adds $23,400 to your annual gross income.

Remember: these are gross figures before taxes. Federal income tax, Social Security, Medicare, and any state and local taxes will reduce your take-home pay. For an interactive calculation that handles your exact hours, try our main overtime calculator.

Key Things to Know About Overtime

  • Federal law (FLSA) requires 1.5x pay for hours over 40 in a workweek for non-exempt employees
  • Some states (California, Alaska, Nevada, Colorado) also require daily overtime
  • Double time (2x) is only required by California state law and some union contracts
  • Holiday pay multipliers are voluntary unless required by contract
  • Bonuses generally must be included when calculating the regular rate for overtime
